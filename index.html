Ada Lovelace - The First Computer Programmer

Early Life

Ada Lovelace was born on December 10, 1815, in London, England.

Ada Lovelace was an English mathematician and writer, chiefly known for her work on Charles Babbage's proposed mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine.

Famous Quote: That brain of mine is something more than merely mortal; as time will show.

Career

Lovelace was introduced to Charles Babbage in 1833, who later developed the Analytical Engine.

She published the first algorithm intended to be carried out by a machine, making her the first computer programmer.

Famous Quote: I am more than ever now the bride of science. Religion to its devotee is marriage; science is ambition.

Personal Life

Ada married William King in 1835, becoming Countess of Lovelace in 1838.

Ada and William had three children together: Byron, Anne Isabella, and Ralph Gordon.

Famous Quote: Mathematical science shows what is. It is the language of unseen relations between things.

Legacy

Ada Lovelace's contributions to computing were not widely recognized until the mid-20th century.

Today, she is celebrated as a pioneer in computing, and Ada Lovelace Day is observed annually to honor women's contributions to science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM).

Famous Quote: Imagination is the Discovering Faculty, pre-eminently. It is that which penetrates into the unseen worlds around us, the worlds of Science.
